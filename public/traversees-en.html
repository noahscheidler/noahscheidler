The Thorn


On a walk in the middle of a blaze. Everything is dying around me. But I observe in the distance, a mist that draws my curiosity. It stands apart from the smoke I have seen throughout my journey through the flames. It is heavier, more convincing, there is something that captivates me. A special element composes it. In a fraction of a second, a path opens before me, a light at the end of the tunnel. So I take it and the obstacles bow before my desire for her.

As I approach, my gaze meets that of a flower, still alive at the edge of the flames. She shimmers with a thousand fires, wrapped in a cold wind whose cover is almost magnetic and bewitching. Perhaps I have ventured into the eye of the cyclone. There before me, probably at the center of this disarray, is the most pleasant warmth of this place.

I do not know whether I should save this flower from this hostile environment or whether her element suits her. It may be that she is the source of it, or perhaps she simply found herself there.

Until now, a torch has protected me from this fire, a stick that ignites to keep the others at bay. The more I brought my torch into this fog, the more the flame that protected me faded before my eyes in the face of the chemical nature of this miracle standing before me. I find myself exposed. I think I am afraid. I no longer need to protect myself. My paradox has found a stronger one. I know my emotions perfectly and for once I am completely lost and guided in the same moment. The confusion transforms into a quest. I know where to go, I no longer want to wander in this hostility. I decide to end it. In the hypothesis that I myself started the fire in which I wander, I found myself face to face with the only being who survived my flames. I would never have found her if nothing was burning. It is time that led me there. Our energies found each other there.

A simple instant of realisation; between the visual contact and the movement of my arm toward her mysterious body. My eyes and a rose. My hand and a thorn. I pluck her from the ground, she is soft. I bleed but my wound is like my incomprehension.

When I picked her, time stopped. The sparks float in the air and the blaze froze like a painting. I hold this rose and these thorns. I am submerged in happiness, I look at this painting and I am seized by fascination. It is the dance of water and fire. The contact of this flower offers me a perspective of infinite creation. From now on I walk through a painting. Static flames, a floating mist and a luminous explosion at the center of the composition. The dance progresses and the work forgets the ordeal of the flames, the light takes hold of the painting which becomes white, almost blank.

There is nothing left around, except my hand holding my rose in its thorns.

I switch the hand in which I hold her. And I sign in red on this blank page the word love.

Noah Scheidler

The Spark


It was a story worthy of a film, a short unexpected episode, in the burning energy of an enigma full of twists

It is a moment I will keep in my mind and that will have destroyed me forever

The light fades, melts, moves away

A little more each time

I am the void, black

She is the comet, fast and furious, the fallen angel of a dream in mourning

Without having had time to speak, it had already defenestrated itself

An abandoned cloud

While I take flight, her wings fall toward the ground

The fog rumbles far beyond the shadows

I was blinded by a light I only glimpsed

Only to become a memory of it

Rubble

Of magic, of chemistry

We simply crossed paths.

Noah Scheidler

The Break


I held back the tears from sliding down my chest

The cold pierced the warmth

The depths of the soul where the flames of the ego reside

The whole pierced my skin

Too late, the cascade carries me away, the birds flee and the clouds haunt me

A downpour approaches

I endure the torment of the seasons

I prepared myself for this flood, all my composure liquefied along my body

Through the flowers and the drink

My clothes and my passions

My dry face became wet with my sighs and cracks

This is the fate reserved for me

For the whim of my feelings

The tears fell

I am nothing more than an excess of them

Noah Scheidler

The Bird


My wings spread

At the window ledge you joined me

Then you left

Capsizing toward the one who made you sail

I am only a shipwreck of my thoughts

A blind man would have seen beneath your laurels

The light I did not know how to witness

Trapped in one of your stray feathers

The wind carried me far away

The wind may bring you to come and retrieve it

To the thief who tore out your heart

This letter the bird never sent

Noah Scheidler

The Penrose Staircase


After the encounter of an invisible step, here we are fallen into an inexplicable void, filled with infinite desires, where as prisoners we make the best of homes. Irreversible is the fall, two bodies floating in the universe of their dreams and stories in reverse. How I love this idea of being plunged into those pupils that give me wings, as if each beat freed me a little more from the illusion of time. How I love this scene carried by the image of having fallen, voluntarily, into our gazes. Never to be hindered again running through the meanders of a staircase without regard.

And in this fall, the seconds twist, the souls approach, the shadows accord, the tears border and our hearts bite, the echoes of our ideas resonate in the void, leaving memories that no impression could erase.

Perhaps we exist only in this vertigo, a suspended illusion, where bodies are at once seized and freed, floating between the imaginary and reality. And in full fall, we rediscover the lightness of being, as if the earth no longer held us, and each beat was our flight toward the unknown.

Noah Scheidler

A Starry Night


I spend the afternoon playing with my little sister, she is two years old, after football she demands her favourite cartoon. An hour later, mum comes home at the start of the evening. I embrace her then help to tidy up. After dinner, I make sure to stay, a sign of hospitality, like a brother, like a son, a presence matters and it has a degree.

I go up to my brother's room, where I sleep before heading back to Paris. I watch a series and an old friend whispers in my ear that I am bored. I think back to my first love who is also my last. The pressure rises and a question returns, followed by an affirmation. How will I live again? I will live again. So I knew that I had lived, therefore I will live again.

I realise I have regrets, not of having lived but of having forgotten to live, when I lived the best. Not having noticed the happiness, which passed me by like crossing a day off a calendar, as easily as a ray of sun pierces the water in a month of July.

The old friend draws a diagram in my head, that of the man who knows how he will be reborn. He hands me in my hand, a leaf to roll, the one that will question my being. It is no longer my habit. Yet the lighter fights with the wind. The smoke dances before disappearing. The red signal is in my hands. It blinks from beginning to end. Consumes itself and converges with my ideas. The addiction will never return for each time it reminds me of my regrets. Yet here I am again playing with fire.

It is dark, I am outside my house. Only the shadows of the trees. Only stars in a clear sky. I spent an hour observing them. Among one of them I saw the one that loves to travel at full speed. I had the chance to formulate a wish. Without any words, in my head an image. A presence I know. Regrets to revoke. A destiny perhaps, my will certainly.

The clouds came to erase everything. This luminous tableau that everyone knows. One by one, their lights no longer reached me, until the last. It shone more than the others, it was the last target of the cloud circle that had taken the others. It let go of nothing, it kept shining. And all around joined it. The clouds could not resist. They returned to the sky the light that was trying to speak to me.

The rebirth. A brilliance that could not give up. The determination of a glimmer of hope transforming into a starry night.

Noah Scheidler

Dust


I have always felt so small

Faced with the world that is vast

As for the universe that is infinite

The stars do nothing but laugh

The light plays tricks on my eyes

Fear plays tricks on my dreams

The rain mocks my moods

The sun feeds on my happiness

The dust gives me this grimace that I am like it

Noah Scheidler
